About

My story.

The short version.

I'm Mikhail. Most people call me Mike. I'm a student, and I build AI agent systems that I actually live with every day.

It started the way it probably started for you: the first time an AI chatbot gave me an answer that genuinely surprised me. The difference is I couldn't leave it there. I wanted to know how far this goes, and reading about it wasn't enough. So I started building.

Today an always-on agent runs parts of my week. I message it on Telegram, in Russian or English, and it plans, delegates to specialist agents, and reports back from its own server, including while I sleep. I built the tools I study with: spaced-repetition flashcards, case simulators, a tracker that starts my mornings. When I want to learn something about AI now, I build something with it.

Along the way I spent a week at a Microsoft office researching a question I keep coming back to: schools pay for AI tools, and students barely touch them. Why that happens, and what would fix it, is the problem I care about most.

This September I move to Cambridge, UK, to start A-levels in Computer Science, Physics, and Maths on an academic scholarship. Everything I learn along the way lands in two places: short daily breakdowns on Instagram, and a twice-weekly email with the tools and prompts that survived my testing.

Away from a keyboard: chess and basketball.
